project got stuck at an impasse, with disagreement among members and the
deadline approaching with no progress. She proactively reached out to every
team member, asked everyone to stay calm and look at the problem
objectively, and helped coordinate everyone's schedules so the work could
move forward. It reinforced for her that handling relationships well is the
foundation of getting anything done, in work or in school, and that teams
need to stay calm under pressure, communicate clearly, and divide labor
sensibly.

On feedback: she sees value in both the positive and the critical. Good
feedback gives her momentum to keep going; critical feedback helps her see
where she's falling short so she can improve. Either way, she treats it as
useful.

On new or unfamiliar problems: she tries to treat them as something to
embrace rather than fear, and to tackle them with courage.

What energizes her: creative work, where she can do something differently
and surprise people, including herself. Repeated failed attempts at
something she's excited about can be frustrating, but it doesn't kill her

creativity.
